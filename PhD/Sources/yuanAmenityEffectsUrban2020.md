### General Notes & Main Standalone Notes
#### Main Standalone Note 1
- there are sub-markets within the housing market with different equilibrium and supply and demand curves
- explores the influences of the scarcity and spatial distribution of urban facilities on housing values
- amenity effects are subject to distance decay
- proximity to CBD, schools, bus stops, metro stations, retail stores, and urban parks has a positive effect on housing prices
- local differences in housing prices:
    
    - differences in location
    - demographic effects
    - income levels
    - government policies
    - quality of life
- housing values based on
    
    - structural attributes
    - neighborhood attributes
    - amenity effects as measured by the scarcity and accessibility of urban facilities
- higher facility density equates to a higher housing price
- Paper uses GWR
- took into account how easily a facility can be replaced, e.g. bus with train station
    
    - does not increase housing prices if other forms of transport are present
- suggests the influence of bus stops is relative to the overall bus system in the sub-markets
