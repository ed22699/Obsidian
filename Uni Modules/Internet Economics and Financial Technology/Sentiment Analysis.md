- sentiment: attitude or option towards X, i.e., how much you like/dislike X
- sentiment polarity: scale on -1 to 1 where -1 is highly negative and 1 is highly positive
- opinions matter, public sentiment can have significant economic value and potential to drive social change
![[Screenshot 2025-12-09 at 14.18.17.png|400]]
- traditional use of focus groups and surveys/polls are expensive and time consuming to run
	- can analyse the vast amount of online opinions to automatically identify public sentiment
- sentiment analysis:
	- process of computationally identifying and categorising opinions expressed in a piece of text, especially in order to determine whether the writer's attitude towards a particular topic, product, or service, etc., is positive, negative or neutral
	- make use of online data: blogs, tweets, reviews, etc, to understand feelings, views, or opinions
		- rapid business decision making (no need for surveys and focus groups)
		- tracking customer concerns
		- spotting opinions that may impact a stock price
		- predicting box-office performance of a new movie
	- *issue*: 
		- more difficult challenge than topic modelling
		- sentiment often expressed in a subtle manner
- sentiment is performed at three levels
	- document-level
	- sentence (or phrase) level
	- aspect (or feature) level
		- opinions regarding any aspect of an object
