- cooled by a combination of three mediums
	- air
	- oil 
	- water
- internal combustion engines work most efficiently when the engine is kept hot
	- engine that runs too cool wastes fuel and won't work efficiently
	- engine that runs too hot will soon melt itself (a theoretical engine that could conserve and put the heat generated to work would have a lot more power and use a lot less fuel, this is why people are looking at ceramic pistons)
- only about 30% of fuel is turned into useful work
	- about 35% excess combustion heat flows out the exhaust pipe
	- the other 35% is absorbed in some fashion by the cooling system
## Air and oil cooling
- sometimes have fins on the cylinders to increase surface area for better cooling
- oil circulating through the engine and air is generally all you need for low and medium performance engines under normal conditions
- matte finishes are actually better than chrome coverings in regard to engine cooling
- clean engine works better than a dirty one
- overheating common when cast-iron used, aluminum alloy was lighter and dissipated heat better
